For pants, fold the waistband flat and measure across: 16.5" equals your 33" waist. If your 36.5" leg figure is the outseam (waist to hem), look for a 30" to 32" inseam; if it is the inseam itself, hunt for tall or unhemmed lengths.

Intentional thrifting

What to look for, in order

1
Structured overshirt or chore coat
In olive, forest green, or dark brown. Workwear brands (Dickies, Carhartt, utility labels) end up at thrift stores consistently. Check both jacket and shirt sections. Fabric: cotton canvas, twill, or corduroy. Avoid nylon or synthetic shell.
2
Heavyweight cotton tees
In near-black, chocolate, ivory, or burnt sienna. Look for 200gsm+ weight. Plain and unfussy reads better than graphic prints near the face. Hanes Beefy, Fruit of the Loom Heavy, or workwear undershirts. Supply is deep.
3
Dark denim, straight or relaxed fit
Raw indigo or very dark wash only. Not medium or light wash. Not skinny, not distressed. Check waist and rise before length. Shortening is easy, fixing waist is not.
4
Burgundy or dark teal knit
Crewneck or zip. Merino or cotton blend preferred. Thrift stores get corporate knitwear in dark tones. Check color, not logo. A plain burgundy crewneck in good cotton is worth more than a branded one in a bad color.
5
Leather or suede belt and shoes in tan, cognac, or dark brown
Matte or semi-matte leather. Thrift stores have leather belts consistently and occasionally footwear. Skip patent or overly shiny finishes.

Pattern rules

Solid or near-solid for anything at face level (tees, crewnecks, collared shirts). Pattern works on a third layer only if the pattern uses palette-compatible colors. A dark green tartan with brown and ochre threads works. A red and white buffalo check does not.

Shoulder seams cannot be altered affordably. If shoulders fit and color is right, that is 80% of the decision.

Lighting check: fluorescent store lighting shifts warm colors cooler and mutes saturation. When a color is borderline, walk it to a window or shine your phone flashlight on it before deciding. Use the tap-to-compare swatches above under that same light.

Fit

For your build

Your shoulders and chest carry width. A relaxed fit in the torso reads cleaner than fitted for most casual contexts. Fitted tees work when the color is strong enough to anchor the look (black, burgundy). Avoid fitted in pale or muted tones.

For outerwear, go one size up from what fits your chest to allow layering and maintain a clean line at the shoulder. The tattoo on your right shoulder reads as a design element. Dark-on-dark layering (forest green sleeve, dark teal jacket) works unusually well because of the contrast between dark ink and warm skin.
